With this piece, I want to depict the idea that perfection doesn't lie in what others think about you, instead it lies in enjoying ourselves and improving day by day rather than just fixing our eyes on the top.


As I stroll past the studio, being
A little bothered by the usual scene
The smiling faces, the gleaming eyes
Yet oddly dull, the picture lies;
The other side of the street, I see
A park with flowers and kids with glee.

Their parents watch them frolicking
Like dandelion seeds in spring
Like lotuses in mud, they mess
Unlike the fancy studio dress
I walk as studio lights retreat,
along the vapidly varnished street.

A sparrow hops on slender stalk
It flies and lands without its flock
I slowly walk to match its pace
It almost falls, but climbs with grace
From tree to tree, it looks for food
We move along in solitude

There stands a man with silver hair
His hands though worn but warm with care
And full of grains, for birds and squirrels
As squeaks and flutter of feathers settle
In just a minute, or maybe two
The clash turns into rendezvous

Behind the man, a statue stands
Of lustrous stone and polished hands
It feels no heat, and neither cold
And nor the sparrow's gentle hold
The statue brightly shone with pride
A light it did not have inside.

I wonder if the statue could
For once endure the greater good
Like nature's love, or sibling’s fight
Or a lover’s painful parting sight
Would it retain its stoic heart
Or shatter in a million parts?

The sun parts from the reddening sky
I close the door, a familiar sigh
And sit to write with a wit to fight
My thoughts, they're there, but lost in light
Instead of being perfect, is it
Perfect to be a human being?
